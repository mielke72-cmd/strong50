🎯 Goals & Approach

Spot reduction isn't possible — but heavy compound lifting + protein + modest deficit will reduce overall body fat, preferentially from your midsection over time. This program prioritizes muscle preservation, which is critical during perimenopause.

🔥 Why Warm Up?

At 50, joints and connective tissue need more prep time. A 5-min dynamic warm-up raises core temperature, lubricates joints, activates dormant glutes, and reduces injury risk by up to 50%. Skip it and you'll feel stiff; do it and your lifts will feel stronger.

🥗 Nutrition Priorities

  • Protein: 100-130g daily (1.6-2.0g/kg)
  • Deficit: 300-400 kcal below maintenance
  • Sleep: 7-8 hrs (cortisol = belly fat)
  • Reduce: Alcohol & refined carbs
  • Hydrate: 2.5-3L water (Dubai climate)

📅 Weekly Schedule

Mon: Workout A (Lower)
Tue: Yoga + 45min walk
Wed: Workout B (Upper)
Thu: Yoga or rest
Fri: Workout C (Full)
Sat: Long walk (60min)
Sun: Full rest

📈 Progression Logic

W1-2: Learn movements, baseline weights

W3-4: +5-10% weight, top of rep range

W5-6: Add a set to main lifts

W7-8: Heaviest weights, drop to 6-8 reps
